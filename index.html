ABOUT ME

I'm a Gameplay and AI Programmer with experience developing gameplay systems in Unreal Engine 5 and Unity.

I enjoy building systems that make virtual worlds feel alive—from enemy AI and procedural generation to multiplayer gameplay and XR applications. My interests lie at the intersection of gameplay programming, AI behavior, world simulation, and immersive technologies.

I am particularly inspired by games such as Dark Souls, Sekiro, Vampyr, Elden Ring, and Where Winds Meet — where AI, systemic world design, and reactive ecosystems create deeply immersive experiences.

What draws me to these games is how they treat the world as a living system rather than just a backdrop.


In Dark Souls and Elden Ring, enemy placement, patrol routes, and AI behavior tell stories without a single line of dialogue — a Hollow's patrol pattern or a Crucible Knight's aggression communicates lore and danger through gameplay. The AI isn't just a combat opponent; it's a narrative device.


Sekiro takes this further with its posture-based combat system, where enemy AI reacts to your offensive and defensive choices in a dance-like rhythm. Every encounter feels like a conversation — the AI reads your patterns, adapts, and forces you to adapt in return.


Vampyr offers a different kind of systemic depth: its NPCs are interconnected through a web of relationships, and your choices as a vampire have ripple effects across the entire district. This is a system that feels alive and responsive, not just a static quest log — a perfect example of world simulation at a narrative level.


Where Winds Meet represents the next generation of open-world systemic design, where dynamic weather, wildlife behavior, and reactive NPC populations create a world that feels truly inhabited, not just populated. It's the kind of systemic density I want to bring into my own work.


These games inspire me to build systems where AI isn't just functional — it's narrative, reactive, and emergent. I want to create worlds where players feel like their actions matter because the systems around them genuinely respond, adapt, and remember.

SmartVR Pointer: Smartphone-Based Interaction for VR
Replaces specialized VR controllers with a smartphone for selection and navigation in VR. Three modalities: tracker-based (QR code tracking), gaze-based, and combined/hybrid. No calibration required — users interact using a device they already know.
MDPI Sensors 2024 — doi.org/10.3390/s24165168
NRXR-ID: Two-Factor Authentication for Extended Reality
Smartphone-based 2FA for XR without removing the HMD. Four challenge types (Checkers, PIN, CAPTCHA, password) tested with 30 participants. Checkers was most preferred and robust across all conditions (p = 0.185); PIN was fastest (avg. 12.35s).
